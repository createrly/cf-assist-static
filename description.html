About

Description

CF-Assist is a global capacity building program that helps key stakeholders in client countries to advance their work on climate finance readiness and low emissions development using a mix of tools including innovative online learning products, knowledge sharing platforms and collaborative leadership programs. The CF-Assist is hosted at the World Bank Climate Change Presidency. The program offers premier knowledge solutions across a range of platforms and modalities to help advance low emission, climate-resilient development strategies and on-the-ground climate actions consistent with national and local development priorities.

Climate change puts communities and investments at risk. Taking into account the various challenges and innovative opportunities in tackling climate change in developing countries, CF-Assist’s strategic work focuses on three priority themes:

  • Climate Finance Readiness
  • Carbon Pricing and Policy Instruments
  • Low Emissions Development Strategies (LEDS)

Climate Finance Readiness

The world’s developed countries committed to mobilize $100 billion a year in climate finance by 2020 to help developing countries adapt to the impacts of climate change and reduce their emissions. Critical to meeting our goals is providing the necessary knowledge and capacity building for countries to more effectively and efficiently access, plan for, and deliver climate finance, otherwise known as climate finance readiness. For most developing countries, this readiness is a phenomenal challenge, and our work enhances learning and knowledge around national policies and programs that prepare developing countries to receive climate finance.

Carbon Pricing and Policy Instruments

Carbon pricing is the foundation for unlocking investment to build an innovative, dynamic economy, and it is gaining in use around the world. It is also good fiscal and economic policy, which raises revenue in an efficient way while encouraging lower emissions. Carbon pricing is appropriate for countries at all income levels. Our work assists countries in identifying various instruments that can be used to price carbon to efficiently and cost effectively reduce emissions, taking into account national circumstances and priorities.

Low Emissions Development Strategies (LEDS)

Tackling climate change requires urgent global cooperation and political leadership to enhance the adoption of low emissions and development strategies (LEDS) that also foster climate resiliency. These strategies provide a foundation to inform and support national and sub-national sustainable development programs and plans that countries are advancing under the UN international climate negotiations. Our work supports identifying, developing and advancing implementation of LEDS as the foundation to sustainable development and climate change action.
